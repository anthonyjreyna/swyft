Selling to us vs. listing with an agent

A cash sale isn't right for everyone. If your home is in great shape and you can wait a few months, listing may net you more. But if speed, certainty, and convenience matter, here's the difference.

Selling to Swyft

  • Zero commissions, zero fees — we even cover typical closing costs
  • Sell completely as-is — no repairs, no cleaning, leave what you don't want
  • Close in as few as 14 days, or pick a date months out
  • Cash — no financing that can fall through at the last minute
  • One walkthrough instead of weeks of showings

Listing with an agent

  • Roughly 5–6% in commissions plus seller closing costs
  • Repairs, cleaning, staging, and photos before you can list
  • 60–90+ days on average from listing to closing
  • Deals can collapse over inspections, appraisals, or loans
  • Open houses and showings on someone else's schedule

Straight answers

Questions homeowners actually ask us

How do you come up with your offer? +
We look at what fully renovated homes like yours have recently sold for nearby, then subtract realistic repair costs and our margin. We show you that math line by line — if a number looks off to you, ask us to walk through it. No black box, no pressure.
Is this a lowball "we buy houses" thing? +
Cash offers are below full retail price — that's true of every cash buyer, and anyone who says otherwise isn't being straight with you. What you're trading for is speed, certainty, and zero costs: no commissions, no repairs, no holding the house for months. For many situations that trade is worth it; for some it isn't. We'll tell you honestly if listing with an agent would serve you better.
What does the 3-day opt-out mean? +
After you sign our agreement, you have three full days to cancel for any reason — or no reason — with a single phone call. No fees, no questions. We'd rather lose a deal than have a neighbor feel pressured into one.
Are there any fees or costs to me? +
No. We don't charge commissions or service fees, and we pay typical closing costs. The offer we write is the amount you walk away with, minus only whatever you still owe on the property (like a mortgage payoff or back taxes, which the title company settles at closing).
Do I need to clean out the house? +
Take what you want and leave the rest — furniture, junk, that garage you've been avoiding. We handle all of it after closing.
What if my house has tenants, liens, or title problems? +
We buy houses with tenants in place, behind on taxes, in probate, or with title tangles. Our title partners specialize in messy files. Tell us what's going on and we'll map out how it gets resolved at closing.
Am I obligated to accept your offer? +
Never. The offer is free, in writing, and good for 14 days. Shop it around — we encourage you to compare. Sellers who compare offers get better outcomes, and we're confident in ours.
